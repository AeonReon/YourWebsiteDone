You Already Know You Need a Website.
So Why Don't You Have One Yet?

If you're running a great local business without a website, you're leaving money on the table every single day. But it's not your fault — the website industry has made it deliberately confusing, expensive, and frustrating.

💸

The Money Trap

Web agencies charge £500–£2,000+ just to get started. Then they lock you into £15–£50/month hosting fees. Even DIY builders like Wix or Squarespace cost £150–£250/year. It never ends.

⏰

The Time Sink

You tried building one yourself. You spent a weekend fighting with templates, fonts, and layouts. The result? Something that looks like it was made in 2009. You have a business to run — not a website to babysit.

🤯

The Complexity Con

Hosting, domains, SSL certificates, CMS platforms, plugins, updates, backups… the web industry buries you in jargon to justify charging more. A local plumber or salon shouldn't need a computer science degree to get online.

👻

The Ghost Freelancer

Maybe you paid someone on Fiverr or found a "mate who does websites." Three months later: radio silence, half-finished work, and you're back to square one — but lighter in the wallet.

🔒

The Hostage Contract

Some web companies don't give you your own website files. Cancel the monthly payment? Your site vanishes. You're renting — never owning. That's their business model, not yours.

📱

The "Just Use Social Media" Myth

Facebook and Instagram are great, but you don't own them. One algorithm change and your reach drops to zero. A website is your digital home — it works 24/7, and nobody can take it away.
